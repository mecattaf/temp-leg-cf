## 2. Uncertainty flags

Things I am not confident about and would double-check before relying on:

- **Workers AI provider list.** "14+ providers" per multiple blog posts, but `developers.cloudflare.com/ai-gateway/usage/providers/` last updated August 27, 2025 — provider list may be stale. Rely on Universal Endpoint semantics rather than a specific provider allowlist.
- **Unified Billing for AI Gateway.** `truefoundry.com` claims it landed in 2026 for third-party models through Cloudflare invoice. I could not find a Cloudflare-first-party blog or doc confirming this is self-serve on Workers Paid vs Enterprise-only. **Flag: third-party source only, not corroborated by Cloudflare docs.** This is SA question #2.
- **Secrets Store cost model.** `blog.cloudflare.com/secrets-store-beta` (April 2025) says "Every Cloudflare account can create up to twenty secrets for free. We're currently finalizing our pricing." Current pricing page as of March 2026 shows 100 secrets/account but doesn't commit to free-forever pricing. **Flag: pricing is beta-tentative.**
- **Workflows v2 concurrency and instance-creation limits.** `workflows-v2` post says limits increased but doesn't name new numbers; original GA had 4,500 concurrency + 100 creations per 10s. **Flag: actual v2 limits unknown.**
- **Browser Run free tier.** FAQ says Workers Free is capped at 10 min/day. Pricing details on paid tier beyond that are on a separate page not directly retrieved. **Flag: paid-tier rate not verified.**
- **AI Search billing start date.** `developers.cloudflare.com/ai-search/platform/limits-pricing/` — "Pricing details will be communicated at least 30 days before any billing begins." Billing start not yet announced. **Flag: open beta, free today, billing TBA.**
- **Cohort 8 timing.** `www.cloudflare.com/startups/workers-launchpad/` shows "Cohort #7 applications are now open" — the cohort recap `workers-launchpad-006` was January 2026, Cohort #6 ran end of 2025. Cohort #7 is current; Cohort #8 dates unstated. **Flag: "Cohort 8" is the next opportunity by naming convention but exact dates are not public.** `noon-ecosystem-support-map.md` claims Cohort #7 applications closed March 13, 2026 and "Cohort #8 should open in the next review cycle — apply now regardless, late apps roll forward." This is Tom's own note, not first-party Cloudflare — plausible but not authoritative.
- **MCP Server Portals plan gating.** `blog.cloudflare.com/zero-trust-mcp-server-portals` says "part of Cloudflare One" — and Cloudflare One Free plan exists (50 users, 50 policies, 10GB transfer) per Zero Trust pricing. So *strictly speaking* Leger could try it on the free Cloudflare One plan. I've rejected on grounds that the governance layer is LP-XX, not CF; but there's a case to be made for "Cloudflare One Free + MCP Server Portals for customer-facing MCP governance" that the SA office-hours call could clarify.
- **Docs contradicting blog.** The docs on Pages are still prominent at `developers.cloudflare.com/pages/` (not retrieved in this research pass), but the migration guide + third-party reporting (Morph, cogley.jp April 2026) are explicit that Workers + static assets is the forward direction. **Flag: official "Pages is deprecated" statement not found; inferred from community signals.**

## 3. Source-reliability notes

- **First-party Cloudflare sources** (blog.cloudflare.com, developers.cloudflare.com, cloudflare.com press releases): treated as authoritative.
- **Third-party analyses** (truefoundry.com, morphllm.com, cogley.jp, theimagecdn.com, spyderproxy.com, roundtable.ai, prosopo.io, nearbound.net, aiquiks.com, controld.com): used for pricing commentary and competitive framing; never relied on for product state or beta/GA claims.
- **Cloudflare Community / forum posts**: treated as user data only, not authoritative for roadmap.
- **InfoQ articles** (infoq.com): treated as well-sourced secondary reporting with dates; used for cross-checking blog post announcements.
- **AI Automation Global / Lushbinary articles on Agents Week 2026**: directional only; they summarize Cloudflare's Agents Week 2026 announcements in aggregate, but I privileged Cloudflare's own `welcome-to-agents-week` and `agents-week-in-review` over these recaps.
